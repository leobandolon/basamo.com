“It’s A Celebration”
By: Marielle Stella C. Pandanduyan

The festival became an annual tradition, fostering a sense of camaraderie among the insects. They shared knowledge about pollination, soil health, pest attack and weather patterns. The honeybees taught the beetles the art of teamwork, while the butterflies inspired the ants with their adaptability.

Seasons passed, and the Great Farm Festival “Pagtipon-tipon sa Uma” became a symbol of cooperation and mutual support. The insects realized that by working together, they could overcome any challenge the farm presented.

One day, a heavy rainstorm flooded the farm, threatening the insects' homes. Without hesitation, the ants, butterflies, bees, and other insects joined forces. They constructed tiny rafts and helped each other reach higher ground, saving their homes and ensuring the survival of their community.

From that day forward, the insects of the farm continued to live in harmony, knowing that the key to their prosperity lay in unity and collaboration. The Great Farm Festival “Pagtipon-tipon sa Uma” became not just a celebration but a reminder that by working together, even the smallest creatures could create a thriving and resilient community.
