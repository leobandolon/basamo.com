Jenny-the-Gennie
Written by Shayne Gian A. Layague

What a great time to be hit by the storm! No stock of foods, only water due to the decreasing numbers of fishes. Lord have mercy! Lola Sita said in disbelief.

Night came, and it started to be a little scary. Winds started to howl, the ocean sounded like it was ready to smash anything in its path, and lightning was seen lighting the dark skies.

"Marialyn! Go to sleep already; tomorrow morning when we wake up, there will be no more storm. Your Lola Sita and I will go out for a moment to ensure our fishing boat is tied properly," Lolo Paquito assured her.

She tried to protest because it’s dangerous, but they assured her that everything would be alright. She then closed her eyes and dozed off to sleep.

The morning came, and everything is back to normal. The ocean was calm, and Marialyn decided to go for a walk and stroll near the seaside, looking for her grandparents since they were nowhere to be found.

She suddenly felt scared and started to overthink.

"What if the ocean took them away? Just like it did to Nanay and Tatay? What if I won’t ever find them again?" she said to herself, crying.

Memories of her started flashing back about how she lost her parents that day. It broke her heart again.

She looked around again and saw many pieces of scattered garbage, and one jar caught her attention.
