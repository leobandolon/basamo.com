One day, a young farmer and a teacher herself named Ma. Lyn, understood the importance of soil health, discovered Angelo's community. Intrigued by the vital role the African night crawler played, Ma.Lyn decided to implement sustainable farming practices in the school.

Ma.Lyn introduced composting and cover cropping, practices that Angelo and his fellow soil organisms greatly appreciated. The school farm flourished with healthier crops, and the soil became more resilient to changes in weather.

Angelo sensing the positive impact of his work, felt a sense of fulfillment. He had become a silent guardian of the land, contributing to the well-being of the farm and the livelihood of those who depended on it.

As the years passed, Angelo continued his humble existence, burrowing through the soil, weaving an invisible tapestry of life beneath the surface. His story became intertwined with the cycles of nature, a testament to the unsung heroes that contribute to the health and vitality of the earth, silently nurturing the world from below.
