“Angelo: The Silent Guardian of the Land”
By: Marielle Stella C. Pandanduyan

In the heart of the rich and fertile soil of a small farm in Eco-Zone, there lived a remarkable creature named Angelo. Angelo was no ordinary worm; he was an African night crawler, a species known for its significant role in enhancing soil health. His life was a testament to the interconnected web of nature that unfolded beneath the surface.

Angelo spent his days burrowed in the warm, nutrient-rich soil, diligently working to aerate and fertilize the earth. His sleek, segmented body allowed him to move effortlessly through the layers of soil, leaving behind a network of channels that allowed air and water to reach plant roots.

As the seasons changed, so did Angelo's tasks. During the rainy season, he worked tirelessly to decompose organic matter, enriching the soil with nutrients. Angelo reveled in the scent of damp earth and the rhythmic sound of raindrops hitting the surface.

When the dry season arrived, Angelo retreated deeper into the soil, forming a protective cocoon to conserve moisture. He patiently waited for the return of the rain, knowing that his work was vital for the survival of the plants and crops above.

Angelo shared his underground world with a diverse community of microorganisms, bacteria, and other soil-dwelling creatures. Together, they formed a symbiotic relationship, each contributing to the overall health of the ecosystem. Angelo's intricate tunnels allowed the roots of plants to reach deeper into the soil, providing stability and access to essential nutrients.
