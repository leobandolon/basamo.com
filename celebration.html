“It’s A Celebration”
By: Marielle Stella C. Pandanduyan

Once upon a time in a productive farm of Eco-Zone, there lived a community of industrious insects who worked together to ensure the harmony of their tiny ecosystem. Among them were a wise old ant named Alex, a carefree butterfly named Blessa, and a diligent honeybee named Charles.

One sunny day, as the insects gathered near the Plant Nursery, Alex called for a meeting. "Dear friends," he began, "I've noticed that our beloved meadow is facing a challenge. The crops are struggling, and our fellow insects are having difficulty finding enough food."

Blessa, with her colorful wings fluttering, suggested, "Why don't we organize a festival to celebrate our diversity and work together to find solutions?"

The idea resonated with the insects, and they set to work preparing for the Great Farm Festival titled “Pagtipon-tipon sa Uma”. Blessa, being a social butterfly, invited insects from all corners of the farm, while Charles organized a team to gather nectar and pollen to share.

On the day of the festival, the farm buzzed with excitement. Beetles danced, ladybugs played music, and fireflies lit up the evening with their enchanting glow. Alex, perched on a clover leaf podium, addressed the crowd.

"In unity, we find strength," declared Alex. "Let this festival remind us that every insect, no matter how small, plays a crucial role in maintaining the balance of our meadow."
