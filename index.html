“Basa Mo, Gulay Ko"

On the onset of the pandemic in School Year 2021-2022, the school adapted the 100% modular modality and the challenge the school has to face is the turn-out of the distribution and retrieval of the Self-Learning Modules (SLM’s) that shows only 63% of the SLM’s were distributed and retrieved. After series of gathering information and feedbacks, it found out that learners and their parents can’t get their modules in school. With this, the school decided to create a program “Module Mo, Gulay Ko”, from the produce of 5,000 square meter school farm. The parents will get and return self-learning modules of their children in exchanged of packs of vegetables. In the first year of implementation, it resulted to 100% distribution and retrieval of self-learning modules.

At the opening of the School Year 2022-2023, the school shifted back to in person classes or face to face modality. At the opening of the classes, the school conducted a pre-reading assessment to 608 learners using the Philippine Reading Inventory (PHIL-IRI) tool. The result show that there were 48 out of 120 Grade 7, 52 out of 109 in Grade 8, 18 out of 97 in Grade 9, 16 out of 96 Grade 10, 3 out of 93 Grade 11, and 4 out of 93 in Grade 12 learners or 23% of the total population belong to the frustration level in reading. With this data, the innovation titled “Basa Mo, Gulay Ko”, new normal approach of the “Module Mo, Gulay Ko” was conceptualized by the innovators to address the problem.
